I Unfollowed Every Influencer for a Month to Save My Bank Account

5 min read

We like to tell ourselves that we're independent thinkers. We think we buy things because we like them. But human beings are mimetic creatures. We mimic. We want what others want. This is an evolutionary survival trait that has been hijacked by algorithms.

The Parasocial Trap

When you scroll through Instagram or TikTok, you're not just seeing products. You're seeing lifestyles. You don't just want the specific beige water bottle the influencer is holding. You want the organized, hydrated, peaceful life that the water bottle represents.

You're not buying an object; you're trying to buy an identity.

This is the "Parasocial Trap." You feel like you know these people. You trust them. So when they say "this changed my life," you believe them. But you're forgetting that their job is to sell you things. They're walking billboards that you invited into your bedroom.

How to Actually Fix It

The Great Mute

You don't have to unfollow your friends, but you need to ruthlessly mute or unfollow anyone whose content makes you feel like you need "more." If an account is focused on hauls, unboxings, or "must-haves," they need to go.

Curate for Creation, Not Consumption

Change your inputs. Follow people who make things rather than buy things. Follow artists, woodworkers, gardeners (who actually garden, not just buy tools), or chefs. When you watch people create, you get inspired to do things. When you watch people shop, you get inspired to buy things.

The Newsletter Purge

Your email inbox is a dangerous place. Every "Flash Sale" and "20% Off" email is a trigger. Unsubscribe from every single retail newsletter. If you actually need something from that store, you will go to the website. You don't need them poking you every morning to remind you to spend money.

Identify the Trigger

Next time you feel a sudden urge to buy something random, stop and ask: "Who made me want this?" Did you want a waffle maker yesterday? Or did you just see a TikTok about waffle makers five minutes ago?
