Laziness Is the Best Financial Tool You Own (If You Use It Right)

4 min read

We're raised to believe that saving money requires iron willpower. We think we need to be disciplined, focused, and constantly vigilant. But let's be honest. Being disciplined is exhausting. Willpower is a limited resource that drains throughout the day.

By the time you get home from work, your willpower tank is empty.

So stop relying on discipline. Start relying on laziness.

The Friction Principle

Corporations spend billions of dollars to remove friction. Amazon patented "1-Click" ordering for a reason. Apple Pay lets you buy things with your face. They want the path from "I want this" to "I bought this" to be a slippery slide with zero obstacles.

You need to put the obstacles back. You need to make spending money so annoying that your natural laziness kicks in and says, "Eh, it's not worth the effort."

How to Actually Fix It

Nuclear Option on Autofill

Go into your browser settings (Chrome, Safari, Edge) and delete every single saved credit card. Do the same for your phone.

The Walk of Shame

Now, when you want to buy that $20 gadget on the couch, you have to physically stand up. You have to walk to the other room. You have to find your wallet. You have to dig out your card. You have to type in the sixteen digit number. You have to type the expiration date. You have to find the CVV code.

Usually, by the time you think about standing up, you'll realize you don't actually want the item that badly.

Log Out of Everything

Don't stay logged into Amazon, eBay, or your favorite clothing sites. Force yourself to type your password every single time. If you use a password manager, make the master password long and complicated.

Unlink the E-Wallets

Disconnect PayPal and Apple Pay from your favorite stores. Force yourself to enter the data manually. Friction is your friend. The more annoying it is to buy, the richer you will be.
